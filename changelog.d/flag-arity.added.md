- `check-flag-arity` (Extras, picked up automatically by the `check-extras` aggregate): flags a `case` arm whose label is a value-taking CLI flag (`-x` / `--xxx` / `--xxx=*`) that reads `$2` or does `shift 2` while relying only on the parse loop's outer `[[ $# -gt 0 ]]` — which proves `$1` exists, not `$2`. Passing such a flag last makes `$2` unbound, so under `set -u` the parser dies with a raw `$2: unbound variable` instead of a clean "flag needs a value". Satisfied by an in-arm arity guard (`[[ $# -ge 2 ]] || die`, `(( $# >= 2 ))`, or a negative `-lt 2` bail), a self-guarding read (`${2:?…}` / `${2:-…}`), or a `need_val` / `need_arg` helper; suppress a deliberate case with `# flag-arity-ok: <why>`.
