- `check-drift-guards` now also catches drift guards by their **copies-agree structure**, not just intent phrasing: a test that reads an external source and asserts a hand-maintained collection (an in-source literal or an `UPPER_CASE` constant / its `.keys()`) equals it is flagged even when it avoids words like "drift guard" (the laundering case — e.g. a test relabeled an "SSOT-coverage contract"). The trigger is deliberately narrow (0 false positives on this repo's own suite) and skips ordinary output-vs-expected assertions; clear a genuine non-guard with a `# not-a-drift-guard: <reason>` comment.
